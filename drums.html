Description:

This portion of the project required me to find some wav files of drums. Take those files and play them back through a wave table. While doing this, I am able to take their original pitch and raise or lower it, I am able to make any drum play at any time and even play two or more drums at the same time. The other big part was I synthesized my own drum using a sine wave. This is all done through a drum instrument file, a drum factory, and a drum envelope. They all work together to load the files into a wave table and then I can freely play them back as I wish. In order to play the drums from an xml file, I mapped each drum to a note. That way, when the xml calls that note, it really calls that drum, so for example, when I call C2, I play a snare drum. You can see the xml format below.

Attributes for the score file:

measure: the measure we are playing in
beat: the beat of the measure we begin the note
note: which drum is playing
pitch: changes the pitch of the drum

Grading criteria:

10 - Waveform playback from tables
20 - Envelope generation
30 - Polyphony
35 - Varying pitch playback from tables
40 - At least 20 distinct drum sounds
50 - Synthesized drum
